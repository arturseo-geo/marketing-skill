# Marketing Frameworks Reference

## StoryBrand (Donald Miller)

The customer is the hero. Your brand is the guide.

### 7-Part Framework
1. **Character** — The customer wants something
2. **Problem** — They face a challenge
   - External problem: The surface-level obstacle
   - Internal problem: How it makes them feel (frustrated, overwhelmed, embarrassed)
   - Philosophical problem: Why this is wrong ("It shouldn't be this hard")
3. **Guide** — They meet a guide (your brand)
   - Show empathy: "We understand how frustrating X is"
   - Show authority: Credentials, results, testimonials
4. **Plan** — The guide gives them a plan (3 simple steps)
   - Step 1: Schedule a call / Sign up
   - Step 2: We build your plan / You get onboarded
   - Step 3: You achieve [dream outcome]
5. **Call to Action** — Direct CTA ("Buy Now") + Transitional CTA ("Download free guide")
6. **Success** — Paint the transformation (before vs. after)
7. **Failure** — Show what's at stake if they don't act

### StoryBrand One-Liner Formula
`[Character] + [Problem] + [Plan] + [Success]`

Example: "Most small business owners waste money on ads that don't convert. We build high-converting funnels in 30 days so you can focus on running your business."

## $100M Offers (Alex Hormozi)

### Value Equation
```
Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)
```

To increase value:
- Increase dream outcome (make the promise bigger)
- Increase perceived likelihood (proof, guarantees, case studies)
- Decrease time delay (faster results)
- Decrease effort (done-for-you, automation, simplicity)

### Offer Stack Construction
1. **Core offer**: The main deliverable
2. **Bonus 1**: Tool/template that removes a common friction point
3. **Bonus 2**: Training or community that increases likelihood of success
4. **Bonus 3**: Speed bonus — helps them get results faster
5. **Guarantee**: Remove risk (money-back, results-based, or conditional)
6. **Urgency/scarcity**: Time limit or quantity limit (must be genuine)

### Naming the Offer
Formula: `[Dream Outcome] + [Time Frame] + [Without Objection]`
Example: "Get 50 qualified leads in 30 days without cold calling"

### Pricing Strategy
- Price on value, not cost
- Anchor high, then reveal the actual price
- Never compete on price — compete on value
- Use payment plans to lower perceived cost

## AIDA (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action)

| Stage | Goal | Tactics |
|---|---|---|
| **Attention** | Stop the scroll | Bold claim, question, stat, pattern interrupt |
| **Interest** | Keep them reading | Relevance to their problem, story, data |
| **Desire** | Make them want it | Benefits, social proof, transformation, emotion |
| **Action** | Get the click/buy | Clear CTA, urgency, risk reversal |

### AIDA Applied to Ad Copy
```
Attention: [Headline that stops the scroll]
Interest: [Why this matters to them specifically]
Desire: [The outcome they'll get + proof]
Action: [CTA + urgency]
```

## PAS (Problem → Agitate → Solution)

1. **Problem**: State the pain clearly
2. **Agitate**: Twist the knife — make the pain vivid, show the cost of inaction
3. **Solution**: Present your offer as the relief

Best for: email subject lines, ad copy, landing page headlines, cold outreach.

### PAS Example
> Tired of spending $5K/month on ads with no leads? (Problem)
> Every month you wait, your competitors are capturing the customers who should be yours. (Agitate)
> Our conversion-optimized funnels generate 3x more leads at half the cost. Book a free audit. (Solution)

---

## BAB (Before → After → Bridge)

1. **Before**: Describe their current painful state
2. **After**: Paint the picture of life after using your solution
3. **Bridge**: Show how your product/service is the bridge between the two

Best for: case studies, testimonials, landing page hero sections.

---

## PPPP (Picture → Promise → Prove → Push)

1. **Picture**: Paint a vivid scenario the reader relates to
2. **Promise**: State what you will deliver
3. **Prove**: Back it up with evidence (stats, testimonials, credentials)
4. **Push**: Close with a clear CTA

## 4Ps of Marketing (Marketing Mix)

| P | Questions to Answer |
|---|---|
| **Product** | What are you selling? What problem does it solve? What features matter most? |
| **Price** | What pricing model? (subscription, one-time, freemium) How does it compare to competitors? |
| **Place** | Where will customers find/buy it? (online, marketplace, direct, channel partners) |
| **Promotion** | How will you reach them? (paid ads, content, email, PR, partnerships) |

## Jobs-to-Be-Done (JTBD)

Customers don't buy products — they hire them to do a job.

### JTBD Statement Format
`When I [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [expected outcome].`

Example: "When I'm onboarding a new client, I want to send a professional welcome sequence, so I can make a great first impression without spending hours writing emails."

### Applying JTBD to Marketing
1. Identify the job the customer is hiring your product for
2. Map the functional, emotional, and social dimensions of that job
3. Write copy that speaks to the job, not the feature
4. Position against the real alternatives (which may not be competitors — could be spreadsheets, manual processes, or doing nothing)

## ICE Prioritization (for experiments)

Score each idea 1-10:
- **Impact**: How much will this move the primary metric?
- **Confidence**: How sure are you it will work? (based on data, not gut)
- **Ease**: How fast/cheap is it to implement?

Average = priority score. Run highest-scored experiments first.

## Pirate Metrics (AARRR)

| Metric | Question | Example KPI |
|---|---|---|
| **Acquisition** | How do users find us? | Traffic, signups |
| **Activation** | Do they have a great first experience? | Onboarding completion, activation event |
| **Retention** | Do they come back? | DAU/MAU, churn rate |
| **Revenue** | Do they pay? | MRR, ARPU, LTV |
| **Referral** | Do they tell others? | NPS, referral rate, viral coefficient |

Focus on the metric that is the weakest link in your funnel.
