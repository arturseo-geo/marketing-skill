## Core Frameworks

### Messaging Hierarchy (build this first)
Before writing any copy, establish:
1. **Who**: Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) — role, company size, pain, goal
2. **Problem**: The specific pain, cost of the problem, why it matters now
3. **Solution**: What you offer + the mechanism (how it works)
4. **Proof**: Stats, testimonials, case studies, credentials
5. **CTA**: The single next step you want them to take

### Positioning Statement
Before any campaign, nail this:
> For [target audience] who [situation/need], [product] is a [category] that [key benefit]. Unlike [competitor/alternative], we [differentiator].

### Value Proposition Canvas
| Customer Side | Product Side |
|---|---|
| Jobs to be done | Products & services |
| Pains | Pain relievers |
| Gains | Gain creators |

Map each customer pain/gain to a specific product feature. If a feature does not map to a pain or gain, question whether to promote it.

## Audience Personas

When building a persona, capture:
- **Name + Role**: e.g., "Marketing Manager Maria"
- **Demographics**: Age range, company type/size, seniority
- **Goals**: What does she want to achieve?
- **Pain Points**: What keeps her up at night?
- **Objections**: Why might she NOT buy?
- **Watering Holes**: Where does she consume content? (LinkedIn, Reddit, podcasts...)
- **Buying triggers**: What event causes her to start looking for a solution?
- **Success metric**: How does she measure if your product worked?
- **Budget authority**: Does she have budget control or does she need to convince a decision-maker?
- **Preferred content formats**: Long-form articles, short video, podcasts, tools?

Output as a one-page persona card.

### Jobs-to-Be-Done (JTBD) Interview Questions
Use when building personas from scratch:
1. When did you first start looking for a solution like this?
2. What was happening in your life/business that triggered the search?
3. What solutions did you try before? Why did they fail?
4. What would make this a perfect solution for you?
5. What almost stopped you from buying?

```
Campaign Name:
Goal: [Awareness / Lead gen / Conversion / Retention]
Target Audience: [Persona name]
Core Message: [One sentence — the "so what"]
Offer: [What are we giving/selling]
Channels: [Paid / Organic / Email / Social]

KPIs: [CPA / CPL / ROAS / CTR / Conversion rate]
```

## Ad Copy

### Google Search Ads (RSA)
- 15 headlines (30 chars max each)
- 4 descriptions (90 chars max each)
- Include: keyword, benefit, CTA, differentiator
- Pin Headline 1 = brand/product; Headline 2 = main benefit
- Use dynamic keyword insertion: `{KeyWord:Default Text}`
- Include at least 2 headlines with numbers/stats
- Include at least 1 headline as a question

### Meta / Social Ads
Formats needed per ad:
1. **Primary text** (125 chars for preview, up to 500)
2. **Headline** (27 chars)
3. **Description** (27 chars)
4. **CTA button** label

Always write 3 variants per ad:
- Pain-focused (agitate the problem)
- Benefit-focused (paint the outcome)
- Social proof / FOMO (what others have achieved)

### LinkedIn Ads
- **Sponsored Content**: Up to 150 chars intro text (desktop truncates at ~150), 70 char headline
- **Message Ads**: Under 500 chars for body, personal tone, single CTA
- **Conversation Ads**: Multi-CTA branching, max 500 chars per message
- Target by: job title, company size, industry, skills, groups
- Best performing: thought-leadership + pain-point messaging
- Avoid: hard sell in first touch; lead with insight or data

### Ad Copy Formula Options
- **PAS**: Problem → Agitate → Solution
- **AIDA**: Attention → Interest → Desire → Action
- **BAB**: Before → After → Bridge
- **4Cs**: Clear → Concise → Compelling → Credible
- **PPPP**: Picture → Promise → Prove → Push

## UTM Parameter Builder
Always include UTMs on campaign links:
```
utm_source    = traffic source (google / facebook / newsletter / linkedin)
utm_medium    = channel (cpc / email / organic / social)
utm_campaign  = campaign name (use-hyphens, no spaces)
utm_content   = ad variant (image-1 / headline-a)
utm_term      = keyword (for paid search)
```
Full URL format: `https://example.com/page?utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=spring-launch&utm_content=pain-variant`

### UTM Naming Conventions
Standardize across all campaigns:
- **All lowercase**, hyphens instead of spaces
- Campaign names: `{year}-{quarter}-{campaign-name}` e.g., `2026-q1-spring-launch`
- Content labels: `{format}-{variant}` e.g., `video-testimonial-a`
- Never put PII (names, emails) in UTM parameters

## A/B Testing
When designing a test, always specify:
1. **Hypothesis**: "Changing X will improve Y because Z"
2. **Variable**: One change only (headline / CTA / image / offer)
3. **Control vs Variant**: Label clearly
4. **Sample size**: Aim for statistical significance (min. 100 conversions per variant)
5. **Duration**: Run for full business cycles (min. 2 weeks)
6. **Primary metric**: One KPI to declare a winner
7. **Confidence level**: 95% minimum before calling a winner

### What to Test (by impact)
| Priority | Element | Typical Lift |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Offer/pricing | 20–100%+ |
| 2 | Headline / hero copy | 10–50% |
| 3 | CTA text and placement | 5–30% |
| 4 | Social proof placement | 5–20% |
| 5 | Form length | 5–15% |
| 6 | Images/video | 5–15% |
| 7 | Page layout | 3–10% |

## Go-To-Market (GTM) Plan
For a product launch, structure as:
1. **Launch goal** (revenue target / signups / awareness)
2. **ICP** (who we're targeting first)
3. **Positioning** (what category, vs. whom, why us)
4. **Pricing** (anchoring strategy, tiers)
5. **Channels** (ranked by expected ROI)
6. **Launch timeline** (pre-launch / launch week / post-launch)
7. **Content plan** (what content pieces, where, when)
8. **Partnerships / PR** (co-marketing, media, influencers)
9. **Success metrics** (week 1 / month 1 / quarter 1 KPIs)

## CRO (Conversion Rate Optimization)
Landing page audit checklist:
- [ ] Headline matches ad promise (message match)
- [ ] Value prop clear within 5 seconds
- [ ] Single, prominent CTA (above the fold)
- [ ] Social proof present (logos, testimonials, numbers)
- [ ] No navigation bar (for campaign-specific pages)
- [ ] Form fields minimized (ask only what's needed)
- [ ] Mobile-optimized
- [ ] Page load < 3s
- [ ] Trust signals (SSL, money-back, reviews)
- [ ] Objection handling near CTA (FAQ, guarantee)
- [ ] Exit intent offer for high-value pages
- [ ] Progress indicators on multi-step forms

### CRO Prioritization (ICE Framework)
Score each test idea 1–10 on:
- **Impact**: How much will this move the needle?
- **Confidence**: How sure are you this will work?
- **Ease**: How easy is it to implement?

Average score = priority ranking. Run highest-scoring tests first.

## Email Marketing

### Email Sequence Types
| Sequence | Trigger | # Emails | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Welcome | Sign-up | 5–7 | Daily for 3 days, then every 2 days |
| Nurture | Lead magnet download | 5–10 | Every 2–3 days |
| Cart abandonment | Abandoned cart | 3 | 1hr, 24hr, 72hr |
| Onboarding | Trial start | 7 | Day 0, 1, 3, 5, 7, 10, 14 |
| Re-engagement | 30 days inactive | 3 | Day 0, 3, 7 |
| Win-back | Churned | 3 | Day 30, 60, 90 |

### Cold Email Rules
- 5–7 sentences max
- Personalize the first line (reference their company, role, or recent event)
- One ask per email (never two CTAs)
- Follow-up: Day 3, Day 7, Day 14 (breakup email)
- Warm up new domains for 2–4 weeks before outreach
- Keep daily volume under 50 per inbox

## StoryBrand Framework (Donald Miller)
Use when writing homepage, about page, or hero copy:
- **Character**: The customer (not the brand) is the hero
- **Problem**: External (surface) → Internal (how it makes them feel) → Philosophical (why it's unjust)
- **Guide**: Your brand — shows empathy + authority
- **Plan**: 3 simple steps to work with you
- **CTA**: Direct ("Buy Now") + Transitional ("Download free guide")
- **Success**: Paint the transformation
- **Failure**: What's at stake if they don't act

## $100M Offers Framework (Alex Hormozi)
Use when crafting offers, pricing pages, or value propositions:
- **Dream Outcome**: What does the customer ultimately want?
- **Perceived Likelihood**: Why will this actually work for them?
- **Time Delay**: How fast will they see results?
- **Effort & Sacrifice**: How easy is it?
- Value Equation: `(Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay x Effort)`
- Stack bonuses, guarantees, and urgency to increase perceived value without lowering price

### Offer Stack Construction
1. Core offer (the main thing)
2. Bonus 1: Tool/template that removes friction
3. Bonus 2: Training/community that increases likelihood
4. Bonus 3: Speed bonus (get results faster)
5. Guarantee: Risk reversal (money-back, results-based, or hybrid)
6. Urgency/scarcity: Deadline or limited quantity (must be real)

See `references/frameworks.md` for all frameworks in detail.

## Scorecard Marketing (lead generation via quizzes)
- Create a 10-20 question assessment in your niche (e.g., "Rate your marketing maturity")
- 5 scoring categories → personalized results page
- Gate results with email opt-in
- Follow up with segmented email sequence based on score

## Churn Prevention
1. Exit survey: Capture reason (price / missing feature / not using / competitor)
2. Pause option: Offer 1-3 month pause before cancel
3. Save offer: Discount or plan downgrade based on exit reason
4. Failed payment: 3-email dunning sequence
5. Win-back campaign: 30/60/90 days post-churn

---

## Onboarding Optimization (Time-to-Value)
- Map the activation event (action correlated with retention)
- Remove all friction before that event
- Trigger activation emails at 1hr, Day 3, Day 7 if event not hit
- Measure: Day 1 / Day 7 / Day 30 retention rates

## Marketing Psychology Principles
| Principle | Application | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Social proof | Testimonials, user counts, logos | "Join 10,000+ marketers" |
| Scarcity | Limited spots/time (must be real) | "Only 3 spots left this month" |
| Reciprocity | Free value before the ask | Free tool, template, or audit |
| Authority | Credentials, media mentions | "Featured in Forbes, TechCrunch" |
| Loss aversion | Frame as what they lose by not acting | "You're losing $X/month without this" |
| Specificity | Use exact numbers over vague claims | "47% increase" beats "significant improvement" |
| Anchoring | Show the high price first, then the deal | "$2,000 value — yours for $497" |
| Commitment | Start with small yeses before the big ask | Free trial → paid plan → annual |
| Contrast | Place options side by side to guide choice | 3-tier pricing with highlighted "popular" plan |

## Free Tool / Lead Magnet Strategy
High-converting ideas: ROI calculator, site grader/audit, template generator, quiz/assessment.
Distribution: dedicated landing page + Product Hunt + SEO for "[niche] calculator" keywords.

### Lead Magnet Hierarchy (by conversion rate)
1. **Tools**: Calculators, graders, generators (highest conversion)
2. **Templates**: Swipe files, spreadsheets, frameworks
3. **Quizzes**: Assessments, scorecards
4. **Reports**: Industry data, benchmarks
5. **Guides**: Ebooks, whitepapers (lowest conversion but highest perceived value)
